Personal Portfolio

Website design

As a university project I was asked to create a website that included all the stages of a project, from research to development. Creating a personal porftolio website was something I wanted to do and I believe websites should have a purpose. The purpose of this website is to display my work and allow me network opportunities.

Tools used: Figma, Photoshop, visual studio code

Year: 2020

Success Criteria

  • Number of website visits - This number can be refined with Google Analytics to evaluate at what point users drop off the website. This extra information can indicate navigation problems on the website.
  • Number of CV downloads - Users have on the ‘About’ section information about my most recent education/work experience, along with the option to download the full CV. This is relevant for users who will be viewing the website on mobiles with data limitations. The number of downloads will give me an estimate of how many users are interested in the full CV, this data is important in comparison with the total number of visits to the ‘About’ page.
  • Number of contacts through social media - This number will give an idea of the percentage of the users that visited my website, are interested and took the time to connect with me.
  • Number of forms submitted - As there will not be direct email available to contact me on the website, the only options the users have to contact me is through social media and forms submitted. Contrary to social media, users will not have to leave the website to contact me through the form. Giving me better information on how many users are interested in contact me against the total number of page views.

Target users

  • Recruiters or companies looking for new employees
  • Other Designers looking for inspiration

Objectives of the user research & user research

My user research is focused on my target users, both designers and recruiters. As the goals of this website are creating new network opportunities and having access to new job offers, what I am looking to find out from the users is:

  • Understanding what users look for in a design online portfolio,
  • Understand if it is useful to have a download button with a full version of the cv,
  • Understand in terms of people who work in the design industry and other designers, what kind of information they look for in an online portfolio,
  • Understand if people find it useful to have a little blurb of information about me, previous job experience and education,

I have conducted my research using an online survey. This method was my first option, due to its nature, it is designed to be done without the researcher having to be present, is similar to interviews and can reach more users by allowing the users to complete the survey on their own time. I have tried to provide a short survey to get the maximum amount of people possible and to avoid them getting exhausted and dropping out.

These two target audiences have overlapping interests, that will need to be present on the website, such as what tools I have used for a project (Photoshop, Illustrator, Figma) and what kind of project it was (branding, ads, user interface). This can be achieve tagging the projects and allowing users to search by tags. This makes it easier for the user to navigate through the website, allowing the users to only see the type of projects they are interested in. However, this hypothesis would have to be tested on a prototype stage, to see if users find it useful or not.

Survey and design decisions

One of the questions was trying to understand how social media can generate website engagement, for this, I have asked if users follow designers they are interested in on social media, 14 people said yes, however, this being a closed question it did not indicate where would be the best placement for the links.

This based my design decision of having the social media links at the top of the contacts page, making it easier for people to see it, as well as having it on the footer of all the pages.

Two questions that were directly related with the design were if users felt like a brief resume should be available without any download option and if there should be a download option for a full cv, the majority of the users replied yes to both. This was reflected on the design of the “about” page where I inserted a download button for the users who are interested in finding information about my full work and education history, but also made available a blurb of my most recent jobs and education for users who could not download the full version.

Conclusions

During this project, it was clear how much impact user research had on the overall product. The main approach used was a user-first approach as this is essential to make a website that was pleasant to the user. Usability and easy interaction were a key focus, people have different needs and merging those needs with the website goals was essential to maximise the potential new opportunities. It was also a reflection of a product life cycle and how small changes and improvements can make a big difference in the overall user experience.

There is not a defined finish date for this project, as time goes on, new tools and techniques come out allowing me to collect new data using different tools such as google analytics. This data gets more and more refined with time and with the total amount of users increasing, allowing the website to be refined based on the data collected.
